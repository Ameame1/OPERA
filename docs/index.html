OPERAAAAI 2026

A Reinforcement Learning–Enhanced Orchestrated Planner-Executor Architecture for Reasoning-Oriented Multi-Hop Retrieval.

Existing RAG pipelines retrieve first and reason after. OPERA fuses the two: a hierarchical three-agent architecture reasons while retrieving — every retrieval step is shaped by the current reasoning state, and every reasoning step drives the next retrieval — trained end-to-end with a new multi-agent variant of GRPO.

Overview

Retrieval and reasoning belong in the same loop

Consider: “What was the previous occupation of the person who succeeded the founder of the company that acquired WhatsApp?” Answering demands a precise sequence of retrievals and inferences, each depending on the last. Conventional retrieve-then-reason pipelines break down here for three reasons: rule-based decomposers produce brittle plans, query reformulation is too shallow to escape failed-retrieval loops, and golden documents drown in noisy top-k results. All three trace back to one root cause — weak coupling between retrieval and reasoning.

Why OPERA

Retrieve-then-reason vs. reason-while-retrieving

✕ CONVENTIONAL RAG
  • Static plans. Rule-based or planner-first decomposition collapses on out-of-template questions and cannot adapt mid-flight.
  • Blind retries. Failed retrievals trigger shallow query tweaks, looping without ever reaching the golden documents.
  • Noise wins. Even when the right document is fetched, coarse filtering leaves it buried in noisy top-k context.
✓ OPERA
  • Reasoned plans. A dedicated Plan Agent decomposes the question into ordered sub-goals with explicit placeholder dependencies.
  • Reasoned retrieval. A specialised Rewrite Agent reformulates the query only when evidence is judged insufficient — trained on retrieval effectiveness (NDCG) itself.
  • Reasoned filtering. The Analysis-Answer Agent assesses sufficiency and extracts the precise answer before the pipeline moves on; a Trajectory Memory Component records the rationale of every action.

Architecture

Two modules, three specialised agents

GPM · Goal Planning Module

Plan Agent (Qwen2.5-7B)

Decomposes a multi-hop question into coherent, executable sub-goals with placeholder dependencies — strategy, decided once and revised only when execution demands it.

REM · Reason-Execute Module

Analysis-Answer Agent (Qwen2.5-7B)

Judges whether retrieved evidence suffices (φ), and if so extracts a precise answer for the current sub-goal — reasoning-guided filtering instead of blind top-k stuffing.

REM · Reason-Execute Module

Rewrite Agent (Qwen2.5-3B)

Activates only on insufficiency: reformulates the query from the reasoning state and feedback, then hands back to retrieval — turning failed lookups into successful ones.

  1. Plan. The Plan Agent decomposes the question into ordered sub-goals.
  2. Retrieve. BGE-M3 fetches top-5 evidence from the merged 1.78M-document FAISS index.
  3. Analyse & answer. The Analysis-Answer Agent answers the sub-goal when evidence is sufficient.
  4. Rewrite on failure. The Rewrite Agent reformulates the query only after insufficient evidence.
  5. Synthesise. Executed sub-goal answers are combined into the final answer; the TMC keeps the full audit trail.

MAPGRPO

Train each agent for the job it actually does

Multi-Agents Progressive Group Relative Policy Optimization extends GRPO to a team of heterogeneous agents: agents are optimised sequentially — Plan, then Analysis-Answer, then Rewrite — each conditioned on its already-trained predecessors, with a reward tailored to its role:

Stage 1

Plan quality

Accuracy 30% · coherence 25% · executability 25% · dependency 10% · efficiency 10% — judged against pre-scored golden plans.

Stage 2

Reasoning accuracy

0.65 × answer EM + 0.25 × sufficiency judgement + 0.10 × format — precise credit for precise answers.

Stage 3

Retrieval effectiveness

√NDCG@k against golden documents + format compliance — the reward is the retrieval outcome.

Two ingredients keep training stable: expert injection (each group of G=8 candidates includes the best sample from a DeepSeek-R1 pre-scored pool, provably reducing policy-gradient variance) and group-relative advantages with a KL constraint. MAPGRPO converges to local optima at rate 𝒪(1/√T).

What the ablations say

On MuSiQue, architecture beats training: removing the Plan Agent collapses EM from 39.7 to 17.1 — below the untrained CoT baseline — and removing Plan & Rewrite together lands at 16.7. Training follows a clean staircase: CoT 21.2 → SFT 24.3 → GRPO 34.8 → MAPGRPO 39.7 EM. Out of domain, MAPGRPO keeps its flexibility where SFT overfits: 36.6 EM on single-hop NQ (SFT: 19.5) and 55.7 EM on MultiHopRAG.
